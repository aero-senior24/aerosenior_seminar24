Our
project:

Surface Modifications For Boundary Layer Control in Propellers

- Proposed Problem statement: Current designs on propeller wings face significant aerodynamic inefficiencies due to turbulent boundary layer from along the blades. This leads to increase in drag, higher fuel consumption and reducing overall performance.


- Objective: To find optimal boundary layers to improve flight performance by exploiting different geometric shapes.


- Approach: We are modeling the propellers along with the riblets on SolidWorks. The resin printer of the A3RG group is used to print our models. We will mount these models on a multi-axis sensor to measure torque and acceleration in order to determine the efficiency of the propeller.

    November 11 2024

    We had our first SolidWorks model for our propeller. This model was unfortunately invalid because the gap in between the riblets was not the same. Based on our mentor directions, the gap has to be the same. After Dr. Narsipur advice, we decided to go for triangular-shaped riblets.

  • Second SolidWorks model

    November 25 2024

    We had our second prototype printed. Unfortunately, the blades are brittle and very easy to break due to the very small thickness of the 10x5.5 propeller. Therefore, we decided to go with a bigger and thicker propeller: a 12x6 propeller.

  • Winter break

    Before next semester starts, we are going to model 3 different propellers on SolidWorks: propellers that have riblets that span over 25%, 50% and 75% of the propeller blade respectively. Testing will take place the whole month on January along with the analysis. The code for the analysis will be provided to us. The whole month of February will be dedicated to the writing of our final AIAA report.
